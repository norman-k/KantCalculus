History of the Computer

Welcome to the History of the Computer by Daniel Kanter

A computer was invented for the purpose of carrying out operations and programs that us humans were just too lazy to do by hand. It is a machine that has revolutionized how everything works in the modern world, including homework, projects, applications, communication research, etc. I believe one of the most important figures to the invention of the computer had to have been Charles Babbage who is considered the "Father of the Computer". He began with the concept of a programmable computer and is credited with inventing the first mechanical computer which eventually led to different designs and thus the creation of the modern computer. Soon came Konard Zuse who invented the first freely programmable computer in 1936. This began the age of mechanization and thus the development of the computer.

This eventually led to development of new memory systems and file storages such as the floppy disk which was called "floppy" for its known flexibility. With this the internet and networking came into power (Networking was invented by Xerox which is currently still in existence as a printing company). Now the competition began and started with Apple vs. PC / Microsoft. Microsoft created the MS- DOS computer operating system in 1981 which Apple rivaled with the creation of Lisa, the first computer ever with a graphical user interface. This led to the development of applications which Windows eventually adopted because it is much more convenient to communicate information through images rather than text commands. Finally, in 1989, the World Wide Web was invented which led to mass research and communication between people. Then the MAC OS system was released and the friendly rivalry between Microsoft and Apple continues to this day. Even though there were disputes, for the sake of the computer, almost 300,000 computers were shipped AROUND THE WORLD in ~2008 which was huge and this led to the modern day of building and creating our own programs and building our own computers.
